Under the Desk: A World of Its Own

Childhood Years

      There are some sounds that I can only recall.1 A specific type of quiet that is unique to memories.2 A certain smell and a string of pictures flash through my head, transporting me to a time when I was younger and life was simpler.3 I used to be a calm, reserved kid who enjoyed quietly observing the world, if I may say so.4 No matter where my mother went, I would follow her.5 I used to find a little spot where I could lose myself in my own world while she worked. 6 Even though we were in the same room, I lived in my own world and never gave much thought to the people or the commotion around me. 8 I placed my pens and coloring book beneath her desk. 9 For six-year-old Mikee, starting school was quite difficult because I was used to spending all of my time with my parents. 10 Since my teachers and classmates were all strangers to me, I would cry because I was afraid of their odd glances. 11 To get through first grade, I brought a big teddy bear to class, I called it teddy. 12 Teddy became the only friend I talked to all day—it was like my safe space.13 Whenever I saw it sitting beside me, I felt protected and calm, as if it was an angel who looked after me. 14 Until I realized, there’s so much to explore in the world - there’s no reason for me to hide in the dark. 15 As the sun sank below rooftops, I changed into a joyful, carefree child who enjoyed running down familiar streets. 16 I started chasing sunsets like as a child. 17 Walang ibang iniintindi kundi ang habulin ang araw at maglaro. 18 Tumakbo man sa ilalim ng init at lilim, umuwi man ako nang pawisan o may gasgas sa katawan o umakyat man ako sa mga puno, maglambitin sa bakal at gumagapang sa putikan kasama ang aking mga kaibigan. 19 Back then, I believed everything—the people, the places, even the feelings—would last forever. 20 Now, All I can do is look back and realize that being a little girl was only the start of who I am today. 21

Teenage Years

     My goal as a teenager was to do something special so that people would remember me. 1 But when faced with crucial choices, it was very easy to get lost. 2 Although I already had my teenage years planned out3Society had given me a detailed blueprint for how every adolescent girl should behave. 4 I would frequently lose myself in my own thoughts. 5 I was so busy with so many things and inundated with ideas that I was unable to even find the right words.6 The pages of my journal began to wear 7 Small holes developing in the thin paper—tales of boys who drew me in and persuaded me to believe there was a spark. 8 Stories of friendships lost, crumpled like scores of unsent letters on the floor. 9 Tears welled up in my eyes as I remembered invitations that were not extended and remarks that were regrettably expressed—or not spoken adequately. 10 The fading of something once thought to stay forever symbolized my teenage high school years. 11 A time filled with unstable teenagers all attempting to survive the in-between existence of being neither a child nor an adult. 12 Teen drama in my life was like an ever-evolving soap opera with 12 seasons, and my best friends and I were in the front row. 13 My long-term teenage friendships taught me almost all I know about life and love. 14 A embroidery of shared events and dreams woven across our lives, creating patterns of joy, understanding, and unconditional support. 15 In the vivid tapestry of my life, one thread stands out. 16 The connection that I have with my best friends, Juliana, Lara, Resha and Kristina. 17 Regardless of what the world offers us, I know that we always have each other’s backs. 18 Whether it’s about heartbreaks, failed relationships, someone we all despise, random outings, or spontaneous rants about life—it all became more beautiful and meaningful because it was with them. 19 Even after spending seven years as a teenager and now growing up, we will always be each other’s home.20

College Years

     Everyone has hopes and dreams. 1 People fantasize about their future—whether it's becoming a scientist who discovers new and beautiful things or a star basketball player who becomes the highest-paid player in the league. 2 Like everyone else, I also have aspirations: dreams of a wonderful life with plenty of money, a good job, and peace. 3 In reality, my future seemed out of reach, as if it were on the next floor of a building while I rushed up an escalator. 4 The urge to turn around and stroll back down was overwhelming, but the sole direction was to run up, no matter how quickly I ran. 5 That setback reminded me that the future is always hanging. 6 Fear makes itself known—the feeling that I have failed myself, that little girl, and her hopes. 7 I had a plan that I dropped in a puddle of rain, and it was too late to save. 8That is the truth of college life. 9 We must prioritize the future over happiness.10 Just like I chose IT over any art course, even though I felt I belonged in art—where I could contribute my talents for the greater good. 11 "Don't blend in to fit in" was the phrase that helped me survive my first year of college. 12 Looking back to my freshman year, I realize how far I have ventured outside my comfort zone, despite the unknown anxieties that remain.13 As they say, “Oh! College na, IT is the best course, andyan ang future.” 14Maybe it's not a bad idea to choose new things. 15You will learn new things that are beyond your scope. 16Meet people with perspectives different from those you usually encounter, and open your eyes to new opportunities, skills, and environments. 17 Perhaps, in another universe where the stars align and in another lifetime, there is a version of me in that art course I once promised myself. 18 For now, I am a BSIT student, dreaming that when college life ends, this course will benefit me as much as I fought with every bone in my body to get through it. 19Just as some gardens bloom under watchful eyes, others grow in shadows, leaving petals to open cautiously, never certain if warmth will linger or fade. 20
